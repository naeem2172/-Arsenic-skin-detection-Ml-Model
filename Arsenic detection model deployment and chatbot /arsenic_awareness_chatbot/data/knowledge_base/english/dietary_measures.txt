Dietary measures to reduce arsenic exposure: Use safe, tested water for cooking and washing food. Diversify diet - include foods rich in antioxidants (fruits, vegetables) and selenium (may help with arsenic metabolism). Limit consumption of rice and other foods grown in arsenic-affected areas if alternatives are available. Wash fruits and vegetables with safe water before eating. Avoid using contaminated water for preparing infant formula. A balanced diet with adequate protein and nutrients supports overall health and may help the body cope with low-level exposure. Consult a nutritionist or doctor for personalized dietary advice.
