Arsenic-induced melanosis is a skin condition characterized by raindrop-like pigmentation. It appears as dark brown or black spots scattered on the skin, particularly on the trunk, limbs, and sometimes the face. The pigmentation often resembles raindrops on a dusty surface. Melanosis typically develops after prolonged exposure to arsenic-contaminated water (usually 5-10 years of exposure). It is one of the earliest and most common skin manifestations of chronic arsenic poisoning. The condition may affect the palms, soles, and mucous membranes. While melanosis can sometimes improve after arsenic exposure ceases, it may persist or progress to more severe conditions like keratosis.
