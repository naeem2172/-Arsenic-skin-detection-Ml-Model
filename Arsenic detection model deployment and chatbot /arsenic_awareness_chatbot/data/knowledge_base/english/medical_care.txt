Medical care for arsenic-related conditions: Consult a dermatologist or physician for proper diagnosis. Treatment depends on the type and severity of skin lesions. Cessation of arsenic exposure is the first and most important step. Topical treatments (e.g., keratolytic agents for keratosis) may be prescribed. Regular monitoring for skin cancer is recommended. Suspicious lesions may require biopsy. Chelation therapy is used in acute poisoning but is not typically recommended for chronic exposure. Supportive care, wound care for cracked skin, and sun protection are important. Follow your doctor's treatment plan and attend follow-up appointments. Early intervention can improve outcomes.
