Other skin lesions associated with arsenic poisoning include: Bowen's disease (a type of skin cancer that appears as scaly patches), squamous cell carcinoma, and basal cell carcinoma. Non-melanoma skin cancers are significantly more common in people with chronic arsenic exposure. Additional skin changes may include hyperpigmentation (darkening) or hypopigmentation (lightening) in patches, and Mee's lines (white horizontal lines on fingernails). Some individuals may experience skin dryness, itching, or increased sensitivity. Any persistent or changing skin lesion should be evaluated by a healthcare provider. Early medical consultation can help with proper diagnosis and treatment planning.
