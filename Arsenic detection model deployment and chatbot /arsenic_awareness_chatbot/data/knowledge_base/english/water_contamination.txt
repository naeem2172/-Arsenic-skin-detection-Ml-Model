Arsenic in drinking water is the primary cause of chronic arsenic poisoning in many regions, including Bangladesh, India, and parts of Southeast Asia. Groundwater from tube wells often contains naturally occurring arsenic that has leached from rocks and sediments. The World Health Organization recommends a maximum of 10 micrograms per liter (µg/L) of arsenic in drinking water. Testing water regularly is essential, especially for well water. Water filters certified to remove arsenic (reverse osmosis, activated alumina) can reduce exposure. Boiling water does NOT remove arsenic; it may even concentrate it. Use tested safe water sources for drinking and cooking to prevent arsenic-related health effects.
