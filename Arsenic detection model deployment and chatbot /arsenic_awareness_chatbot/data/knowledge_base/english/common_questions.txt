Common questions about arsenic: Q: How do I know if my water has arsenic? A: Get your water tested by a certified laboratory. Test kits are available in some areas. Q: Can arsenic poisoning be cured? A: Stopping exposure is essential. Some skin changes may improve; others may persist. Medical management can help. Q: Is it safe to bathe in arsenic-contaminated water? A: Skin absorption is low, but avoid using contaminated water for drinking or cooking. Q: How long does exposure take to cause symptoms? A: Typically 5-10 years of regular exposure for skin changes to appear. Q: What should I do if I suspect arsenic exposure? A: Switch to safe water immediately, and consult a healthcare provider for evaluation.
