Sources of arsenic exposure include: contaminated drinking water (most common), food cooked with arsenic-contaminated water, rice and other crops irrigated with contaminated water, industrial exposure (mining, smelting, pesticides), and certain traditional medicines. Long-term exposure through drinking water is the main concern for skin manifestations. The risk increases with the concentration of arsenic and duration of exposure. Children and pregnant women may be more vulnerable. Reducing exposure from all sources is important for prevention and management of arsenic-related health effects.
