Arsenic-induced keratosis involves hardening and thickening of the skin, most commonly on the palms of the hands and soles of the feet. The skin becomes rough, dry, and may develop raised lesions or nodules. Palmar keratosis appears as small, corn-like growths on the palms, while plantar keratosis affects the soles. These lesions can be painful and may crack or bleed. Keratosis typically develops after melanosis and indicates more advanced chronic arsenic poisoning. In some cases, keratosis can progress to skin cancer (Bowen's disease or squamous cell carcinoma). Early detection and cessation of arsenic exposure are important. Medical evaluation by a dermatologist is recommended for proper diagnosis and management.
